## definition
Abilities-as-apps refers to a conceptual framework where various capabilities and features within the OpenHome platform are treated as individual applications. This approach allows users to access specific functionalities in a modular and user-friendly manner.

## why-it-matters
This concept is crucial for enhancing user experience by providing tailored interactions with the platform. By segmenting abilities into discrete applications, users can easily customize their home automation systems, ensuring that each feature can be utilized independently or integrated as needed. This promotes a more flexible and efficient environment that adapts to user preferences.

## how-it-manifests
The abilities-as-apps concept appears throughout the OpenHome platform as distinct modules or applications that users can enable or disable according to their needs. This can include features like lighting control, temperature regulation, security measures, and entertainment options, all available through a centralized interface, making it easier for users to manage their home environment.
