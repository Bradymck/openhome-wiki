### definition
The concept of "abilities-as-apps" refers to the approach of treating individual functionalities or capabilities within a platform as discrete applications that can be independently accessed, managed, or executed.

### why-it-matters
This concept is significant for OpenHome as it allows for greater flexibility and customization for users. By enabling abilities to function as separate applications, users can tailor their experiences and interactions within the OpenHome ecosystem, making it more user-centric and adaptable to varied needs.

### how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, the "abilities-as-apps" approach can manifest through a modular architecture where different functionalities—such as home automation, device management, or information querying—are encapsulated as independent applications. This allows users to pick and choose which abilities they want to utilize, promoting a more personalized and efficient use of the platform.
