### definition
Local-first refers to a development approach where applications prioritize executing functions and storing data locally on devices rather than relying heavily on cloud services. This ensures that users have access to capabilities even when they are offline or have limited connectivity.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first architecture enhances user experience by providing more responsive interactions with AI agents while maintaining user privacy. It minimizes dependence on external networks, which can be unpredictable, and allows seamless operation in various environments.

### how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, local-first principles are evident in how the platform's smart devices function. These devices can engage in voice interactions, process commands, and manage local data without needing continuous internet access, ensuring reliability and-user control.
