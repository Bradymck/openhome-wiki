## definition
Local First refers to a technology paradigm that emphasizes local data storage and processing on devices, prioritizing offline functionality and user autonomy. This approach allows applications to continue functioning even without a network connection, ensuring uninterrupted access and response.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, Local First is crucial as it supports the platform's mission of creating a reliable and responsive voice AI experience. By leveraging local capabilities, OpenHome can offer seamless interactions, enhance user privacy, and minimize dependency on internet connectivity. This approach also empowers developers to build applications that work effectively in diverse environments.

## how-it-manifests
Local First manifests in OpenHome through its architecture and functionalities, where voice AI agents can operate independently of the cloud. For instance, smart speakers built on the platform use local processing to execute commands, control devices, and manage interactions without the need for constant internet connectivity. This ensures that users can access their AI assistants anytime and anywhere.
