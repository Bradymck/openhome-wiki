## definition
Local First refers to a computing paradigm where applications prioritize local data processing and storage over reliance on cloud services. This approach ensures that essential functionalities can be performed offline, creating a more resilient user experience.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the Local First concept enhances the functionality of AI agents by allowing them to operate independently of internet connectivity. This is crucial for users in areas with unreliable access to the internet and helps in ensuring user privacy by limiting data transfers to the cloud.

## how-it-manifests
Local First manifests in OpenHome through its physical DevKits, which integrate smart speaker functionality capable of processing commands and data locally. This allows AI agents, also known as Personalities, to interact seamlessly with users through voice recognition and response, even in offline scenarios. Abilities function as local plugins that provide additional skills without needing constant internet access.
