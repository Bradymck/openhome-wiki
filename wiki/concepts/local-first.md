## definition
Local-first is an approach that emphasizes the importance of running applications primarily on the user's device rather than relying on remote servers. This paradigm aims to ensure that the application can function autonomously and efficiently, even without a constant internet connection.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first is crucial as it allows for enhanced privacy, reduced latency, and better performance of voice AI applications. By processing data locally, users can enjoy a seamless experience that is less dependent on external service reliability, aligning with OpenHome’s mission to create intuitive and immersive interactions.

## how-it-manifests
This concept is reflected in OpenHome's design of agents, which can operate independently of cloud resources. The physical V0.1 devkits, described as fully local smart speakers, embody local-first principles by allowing users to interact with music, smart home devices, and AI capabilities without needing to connect to external services continually. The platform's ability to deploy apps and run abilities locally showcases its commitment to this approach.
