### definition
Local-first is a design philosophy that promotes the use of local resources and processes over cloud-based solutions. It emphasizes the importance of functionality and data accessibility in devices, ensuring that they can operate independently of internet connectivity.

### why-it-matters
This concept is crucial for OpenHome as it enhances the reliability and user autonomy of smart devices. By prioritizing local processing and storage, users can maintain functionality without a continuous internet connection, which can be vital in areas with inconsistent connectivity or during outages. It empowers users to interact with their devices seamlessly and securely.

### how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, local-first principles are reflected in the operation of smart devices that rely on local data and processes to function optimally. For instance, devices can perform actions like controlling smart home features, playing music, or engaging with voice AI without needing to access cloud services immediately. This approach not only speeds up interactions but also protects user privacy by limiting data transmission.
