## definition
Local First is an approach to software development that emphasizes the importance of running applications and processing data primarily on the user's device rather than relying heavily on cloud services. This concept allows for a more responsive user experience and greater privacy, as operations occur close to where the data resides.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the Local First philosophy enhances user control over their data and interactions with AI agents. By ensuring that capabilities are available locally, users experience improved performance, reduced latency, and enhanced privacy, as their personal data does not need to be sent to the cloud for processing.

## how-it-manifests
Local First manifests in the OpenHome platform through its architecture and design of AI agents. These agents can operate without continuous internet connectivity, leveraging local hardware to enhance functionalities such as music playback, smart home control, and conversational AI interactions, allowing for a seamless user experience even in offline scenarios.
