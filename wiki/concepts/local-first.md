### definition
Local-first is a concept that emphasizes the importance of running applications and storing data primarily on the user's local device rather than relying solely on cloud services. This approach prioritizes accessibility, privacy, and user control over their data.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first is significant because it ensures that users retain control of their data and can access their applications without dependence on an internet connection. This is particularly crucial for fostering a reliable and user-centered environment, where data sovereignty and privacy are paramount.

### how-it-manifests
The local-first concept can manifest in OpenHome through applications that operate efficiently offline, synchronization features that allow for data updates between local devices and servers when connectivity is available, and interfaces that prioritize user interaction directly with their local environments.
