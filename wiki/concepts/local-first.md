## definition
Local-first refers to an approach in software development where applications prioritize local data storage and processing on users' devices over relying exclusively on cloud services. This concept emphasizes user autonomy and data accessibility irrespective of internet connectivity.

## why-it-matters
Local-first is relevant to OpenHome as it enhances user experience by ensuring that access to data and functionalities is maintained even in low or no internet conditions. This approach can foster trust and reliability, key aspects of user engagement in digital home environments.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, the local-first principle manifests through features that allow users to interact with their home environment directly through local applications, enabling them to customize settings and access data without always needing to connect to the internet.
