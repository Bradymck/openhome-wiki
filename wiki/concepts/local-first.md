## definition
Local First is a design philosophy that emphasizes the importance of processing data and performing operations locally on user devices, rather than relying on remote servers. This approach enhances performance, privacy, and responsiveness in applications, particularly in environments where network connectivity may be limited or inconsistent.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, Local First is pivotal as it allows voice AI agents to operate effectively even in situations where internet access is unreliable. By prioritizing local data processing, OpenHome enhances user experiences and maintains functionality, making voice interactions smoother and more reliable.

## how-it-manifests
The Local First concept is realized in the OpenHome platform through the architecture of its smart devices, which can perform tasks such as playing music, controlling smart home devices, and executing voice commands without constant internet connectivity. This local capability means that even when users are offline, their voice AI agents can still function effectively, ensuring a seamless user experience.
