### definition
Local-first is a conceptual framework emphasizing the importance of local data and interactions in digital environments. It seeks to empower users by ensuring that their data resides primarily on local devices, rather than being centralized on remote servers.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first architecture is crucial for enhancing user privacy, data control, and performance. It allows individuals to manage their digital interactions and personal data directly on their devices, promoting autonomy and security while reducing reliance on internet connectivity.

### how-it-manifests
This concept manifests in various aspects of the OpenHome platform, including local data storage, offline functionality, and peer-to-peer communication. Users can access their data and applications without needing a constant internet connection, fostering a more resilient digital environment.
