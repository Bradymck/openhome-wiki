### definition
Local-first refers to a design philosophy where applications prioritize local data storage and processing on the user's device. This approach enhances privacy, performance, and user autonomy by minimizing reliance on cloud services.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first is critical for ensuring that voice AI interactions are secure, responsive, and reliable. By keeping data and processing local, OpenHome empowers users with more control over their data, leading to enhanced trust and user experience.

### how-it-manifests
The local-first concept is manifested within OpenHome through the development of smart speakers that operate fully offline, enabling functionalities such as music playback, smart home control, and interaction with AI without requiring an internet connection. This ensures that users can access their devices and functionalities seamlessly, even in the absence of connectivity.
