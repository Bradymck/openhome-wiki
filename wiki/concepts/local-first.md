## definition
Local-first refers to a development and operational approach where applications prioritize local data storage and processing on a user's device, ensuring accessibility and performance even in the absence of an internet connection.

## why-it-matters
This approach is relevant to OpenHome as it promotes user autonomy by enabling individuals to maintain control over their data and experience. Local-first strategies enhance resilience, making systems more robust against network failures and improving user engagement by offering seamless interactions.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, local-first principles may appear in features that allow users to store and manage their data locally, facilitating immediate access and operations without relying on cloud infrastructure. This could be implemented in various applications, enhancing functionality in offline scenarios.
