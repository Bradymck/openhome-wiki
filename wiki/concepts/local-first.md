## definition
Local first is a design philosophy emphasizing processing and data storage on local devices before syncing with cloud services. This approach prioritizes the user's privacy, reduces latency, and enhances the reliability of applications by allowing them to function independently of internet connectivity.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, adopting a local first approach enhances user experience by ensuring that smart devices can operate seamlessly even without consistent internet access. This is particularly relevant for applications that require real-time interaction and responsiveness, such as voice AI interfaces.

## how-it-manifests
The local first concept manifests in various aspects of OpenHome's platform, particularly in how voice agents (Personalities) and their Abilities are designed to function autonomously on local hardware, such as smart speakers and devices. This means users can access features like music playback or home automation commands without relying on remote servers, leading to quicker responses and improved user satisfaction.
