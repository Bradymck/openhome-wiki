### definition
Local-first refers to a design philosophy and approach where applications prioritize local data storage and processing, enabling them to function effectively regardless of internet availability.

### why-it-matters
This concept is relevant to OpenHome as it aligns with the need for a resilient and user-centric experience. By focusing on local-first principles, OpenHome can ensure continuous functionality and faster response times, enhancing user satisfaction even in situations where network connectivity is poor or nonexistent.

### how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, local-first can manifest in various ways, such as allowing users to access their data, preferences, and settings directly from their devices without needing constant online connectivity. This can include features like offline access, local data synchronization, and device autonomy.
