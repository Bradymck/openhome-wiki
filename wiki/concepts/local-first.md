## definition
Local-first is a concept that emphasizes the importance of enabling applications to operate primarily on the user's local device, prioritizing data sovereignty and offline functionality.

## why-it-matters
This approach is particularly relevant to OpenHome, as it aligns with the principles of user control, privacy, and seamless access to information regardless of internet connectivity. By empowering users to retain control over their data locally, OpenHome fosters a more secure and user-friendly environment.

## how-it-manifests
Within the OpenHome platform, local-first principles may be evident in functionalities that allow users to interact with applications without being reliant on constant internet access. This could include configurations that store data locally while syncing when a connection is available, providing resilience and reliability in user experiences.
