### definition
Local First refers to the principle of prioritizing data processing and interaction on the local device rather than relying on remote servers. This approach ensures that applications can operate seamlessly in offline modes and enhances user privacy and data security.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, Local First is significant because it enables AI-powered voice applications to function efficiently without constant internet connectivity. This enhances user experience by allowing devices to perform tasks locally, leading to faster response times, improved privacy, and greater reliability in various environments.

### how-it-manifests
The Local First concept is evident in the architecture of OpenHome's AI agents, particularly within the capabilities of the physical V0.1 devkits. These devices emphasize on-device processing for voice interactions and smart home controls, allowing users to engage with their AI agents without continuous reliance on cloud services.
