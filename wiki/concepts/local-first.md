## definition
Local-first is an approach that emphasizes the importance of keeping data and processes primarily on local devices, allowing users to maintain control and access to their information without being reliant on constant internet connectivity.

## why-it-matters
This concept is particularly relevant to OpenHome as it aligns with the goals of enhancing user autonomy, ensuring privacy, and facilitating a responsive experience even in low-connectivity environments. By prioritizing local interactions, users can enjoy a more seamless and secure interaction with their homes and devices.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, local-first principles are reflected in various features that allow devices to operate independently while still enabling synchronization and updates when online. This can include local data storage for user preferences, device control through local networks, and the ability to interact with services without requiring constant internet access.
