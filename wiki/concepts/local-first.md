### definition
Local-first is a software development concept that emphasizes the importance of creating applications that primarily operate on local devices, rather than relying on constant internet connectivity. This approach allows for more responsive and reliable user experiences, especially in scenarios with intermittent or poor network conditions.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first principles facilitate better performance and usability for users who may not always have access to fast internet. This is particularly relevant for a platform that aims to be inclusive and accessible to a diverse user base. By prioritizing local functionality, OpenHome can ensure its services remain available even in low-connectivity environments.

### how-it-manifests
The local-first concept appears in OpenHome through features that enable offline access to applications, allowing users to continue interacting with their tools and data without interruption. This may include local data storage, syncing capabilities when the internet is available, and user interfaces that function seamlessly regardless of connectivity.
