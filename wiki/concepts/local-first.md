### definition
Local-first is a design philosophy that emphasizes processing and storing data primarily on the user's device rather than relying on remote servers. This approach fosters greater control, privacy, and responsiveness for users.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, local-first principles are essential as they enhance user privacy and autonomy. By ensuring that data processing occurs locally, OpenHome empowers users to manage their information more securely while providing a more reliable service, even in low connectivity scenarios.

### how-it-manifests
Local-first principles within OpenHome can be seen in the architecture of its AI agents and abilities, which prioritize local data processing and storage. This operational model results in faster response times and reduces dependency on internet connectivity, making the user experience smoother and more efficient.
