### definition
Local-First is a principle in software design where applications prioritize local processing and data storage, allowing for improved performance and responsiveness. This approach enables users to interact with applications in real-time even without a consistent internet connection. 

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the Local-First concept is crucial as it enhances the reliability and user experience of AI agents. By minimizing dependence on cloud services, users can maintain seamless interactions with their devices, fostering a more resilient and efficient environment, particularly in scenarios with limited connectivity.

### how-it-manifests
OpenHome's platform leverages Local-First principles by enabling agents (Personalities) to operate autonomously with local resources. Features such as controlling smart home devices, accessing locally stored media, and processing voice commands occur directly on the device, significantly reducing latency and enhancing user satisfaction.
