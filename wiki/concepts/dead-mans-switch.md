### definition
A dead man's switch is a safety mechanism that automatically performs a specific action if the operator becomes incapacitated or unable to continue. Essentially, it ensures that a system or process cannot be left unattended without oversight, often to prevent unwanted circumstances or failures.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, a dead man's switch is critical for enhancing user safety and system reliability. It can help ensure that home automation systems continue to operate effectively even if the user is unable to intervene, thereby minimizing potential risks associated with unattended operations.

### how-it-manifests
This concept appears in OpenHome in various forms, such as emergency protocols that activate when user input is not detected for a certain period. For instance, if a user falls ill and cannot shut down or alter their home system, the dead man's switch can automatically turn off appliances or alert emergency services.
