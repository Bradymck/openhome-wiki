## definition
A dead man's switch is a safety mechanism that triggers an action if the operator becomes incapacitated or fails to actively control a process. It ensures that a predefined action occurs to mitigate risk or alert others when the operator is no longer able to perform their duties.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, implementing a dead man's switch can enhance safety and reliability for users interacting with smart home devices. It ensures that if a user is unable to respond or take necessary action, the system can automatically execute a fallback or safe procedure, thus protecting both users and devices.

## how-it-manifests
While specific implementations of a dead man's switch within the OpenHome platform are not detailed in the current documentation, the principle could manifest as features that respond to inactivity, alert caregivers, or automate actions in the event of a user’s unresponsiveness.
