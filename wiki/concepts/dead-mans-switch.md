## definition
A "dead man's switch" is a safety mechanism that is designed to automatically trigger an action when a user becomes incapacitated or fails to respond within a certain timeframe. This concept ensures that specific processes or notifications are enacted to manage situations where someone may no longer be able to operate normally.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the dead man's switch can enhance user safety and ensure continuity of operations, especially in critical home management scenarios. It can provide peace of mind for users by ensuring that necessary actions are taken if they cannot perform them themselves, thereby increasing the reliability of home automation systems.

## how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, dead man's switches may be integrated into various applications to monitor user activity or health status. If a user fails to interact with the system (due to incapacitation), the dead man's switch can initiate predefined actions, such as alerting family members, emergency services, or activating secure measures within the home.
