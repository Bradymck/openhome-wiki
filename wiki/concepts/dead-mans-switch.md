## definition
A dead man's switch is a safety mechanism that triggers an action if the user becomes incapacitated or fails to take a specific action over a certain period of time. It is designed to ensure continuity or safety in critical situations, preventing unwanted outcomes.

## why-it-matters
In OpenHome, the dead man's switch concept is relevant for ensuring the reliability and integrity of user interactions and automations. By implementing such switches, OpenHome can enhance user safety and the dependability of automated processes, especially in contexts where user intervention is vital.

The dead man's switch concept may manifest in various features or automations within the OpenHome platform that require user confirmations or actions to remain active.
