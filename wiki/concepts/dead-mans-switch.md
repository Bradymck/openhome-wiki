### definition
A dead man's switch is a mechanism that automatically performs a specific action when a designated operator becomes incapacitated, fails to respond, or is no longer able to control the system. This concept ensures that certain processes either stop or trigger an alert to prevent harm or loss.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the dead man's switch can enhance the reliability and safety of AI interactions. By ensuring that critical processes continue correctly or halt when a user can no longer manage them, it provides a safeguard in scenarios where services (such as voice agents) might be left unattended, ultimately improving user trust and system integrity.

### how-it-manifests
The dead man's switch concept may be applied within OpenHome's ability to monitor and respond to user engagement. For instance, if a user is interacting with their AI agent and does not provide input for a specified duration, the system might activate a backup protocol or safety feature to prevent unwanted outcomes. This could be used, for example, in health-related applications where user safety is of utmost importance.
