## definition
A Dead Man's Switch is a safety mechanism that requires a user to perform an action at specific intervals to maintain operation. If the user fails to act within the designated time, the system takes a predefined action, typically shutting down or activating a failsafe.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, a Dead Man's Switch can enhance user safety and security. It ensures that critical functions or personalized services remain active only when the user is present, preventing unintended operations or access in case of user unavailability.
