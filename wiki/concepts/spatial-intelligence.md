### definition
Spatial intelligence refers to the ability to visualize and manipulate spatial relationships and environments, enabling a person to navigate effectively within physical spaces. This form of intelligence is essential in creating interactions that involve the placement and movement of objects or data in a three-dimensional context.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, spatial intelligence plays a critical role in developing and enhancing user experiences through intuitive and immersive environments. By leveraging spatial reasoning, applications can provide more meaningful interactions that align with users' real-world contexts, thus enriching their engagement with technology.

### how-it-manifests
Spatial intelligence may manifest in OpenHome through features such as personalized home automation setups, where smart devices are aware of their positions and can respond accordingly. This involves understanding the layout of a user’s home, enabling the platform to execute commands based on where objects are located or how they are arranged within a room.
