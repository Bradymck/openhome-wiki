## definition
Spatial intelligence refers to the capacity to think in three dimensions, to visualize and manipulate objects, and to understand the relationships between different spaces and their dimensions. It encompasses skills related to navigation, spatial perception, and the ability to comprehend and create spatial layouts.

## why-it-matters
Spatial intelligence is pivotal for enhancing user interactions within OpenHome. By understanding and applying spatial concepts, the platform can improve how users engage with their environments, making interactions more intuitive and seamless. This relevance is particularly crucial for applications that involve physical spaces, such as home automation, navigation, and augmented reality experiences.

## how-it-manifests
On the OpenHome platform, spatial intelligence is manifested through features that allow users to map and control their home spaces intelligently. This includes functionalities like spatial mapping for smart devices, creating floor plans, and utilizing augmented reality to visualize changes in real-time. Such applications leverage spatial intelligence to optimize user experience and increase operational efficiency.
