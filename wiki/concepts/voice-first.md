## definition
Voice-first refers to technologies and applications that prioritize voice as the primary interface for interaction with digital devices and services. This paradigm shift allows users to communicate with systems through spoken commands, enabling more intuitive and hands-free experiences.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is at the core of OpenHome's mission to revolutionize human-technology interactions. By focusing on voice interactions, OpenHome aims to make AI-powered applications accessible and user-friendly, enhancing everyday life through immersive and natural conversations.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first technology is realized through the use of "Personalities," which are AI agents designed to engage users in conversation. These agents utilize various "Abilities," which are voice-activated plugins that expand their functionality. Users can interact with Personalities using natural language, making it easier to control smart devices, access information, and perform tasks solely through voice.
