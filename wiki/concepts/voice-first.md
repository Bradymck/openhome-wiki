### definition
Voice-first is a design approach that prioritizes voice interaction as the primary mode of user engagement with technology. It focuses on enabling users to interact seamlessly with devices and applications using spoken language, making technology more accessible and intuitive.

### why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is essential to OpenHome as it enhances user experience by allowing for hands-free interaction and improves accessibility for users with varying abilities. It facilitates a more natural way of interacting with devices, aligning with the growing trend of voice-activated systems in everyday life.

### how-it-manifests
Within the OpenHome platform, voice-first concepts manifest through voice-activated commands and virtual assistants that allow users to control home automation systems, access information, and perform tasks without needing a physical interface. This functionality is integrated across various applications and services in the OpenHome ecosystem.
