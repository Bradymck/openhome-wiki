## definition
Voice-first refers to the design and development of applications and interfaces that prioritize voice interactions as the primary mode of communication between users and technology. This concept emphasizes the use of voice recognition and natural language processing to facilitate user engagement seamlessly.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is crucial for OpenHome as it enhances user accessibility and interaction with the platform. By allowing users to initiate commands and interactions through voice, OpenHome creates a more intuitive user experience, catering to users who may prefer or require hands-free engagement.

## how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, voice-first can manifest in various ways, such as personal assistants that respond to voice commands for controlling smart home devices, accessing information, or managing user preferences. Features like speech recognition capabilities and conversational interfaces are integrated into applications, allowing for more natural interactions.
