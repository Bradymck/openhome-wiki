## definition
Voice-first is an approach that prioritizes voice interactions as the primary mode of communication with technology, allowing users to engage with devices and applications through spoken commands rather than traditional interfaces like touchscreens or keyboards.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, the voice-first concept facilitates a more intuitive and accessible user experience. It enables users to interact seamlessly with their home environments, enhancing automation and connectivity. This approach aligns with the growing trend of smart home technologies and caters to varying user preferences, particularly for those who may have difficulty using conventional interfaces.

## how-it-manifests
Voice-first technology appears within OpenHome through the integration of voice assistants and speech recognition systems. Users can issue commands to control smart devices, access information, and manage home settings simply by speaking. The platform may also feature voice-responsive interfaces that recognize natural language, making interactions feel more organic and user-friendly.
