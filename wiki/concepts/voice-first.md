## definition
Voice-first refers to a paradigm in technology where voice interaction is the primary means of interface and communication with devices and applications. In this approach, users engage with technology through spoken commands and conversations rather than traditional input methods, like clicking or typing.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is central to OpenHome's mission of revolutionizing human-technology interactions. It empowers users to connect with their devices in a more natural and intuitive way, enhancing the accessibility and usability of technology for a broader audience. By prioritizing voice interactions, OpenHome aims to create immersive, human-like experiences that integrate seamlessly into daily life.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first manifests through the development of Personalities—AI agents that leverage voice interaction to engage users. These Personalities utilize modular Abilities that respond to voice commands, allowing for dynamic and context-aware conversations. The design encourages users to initiate conversations naturally, triggering specific functionalities based on their spoken requests.
