## definition
Voice-first refers to the design and interaction model that prioritizes voice commands and audio outputs as the primary method of communicating with digital systems, rather than relying on traditional visual interfaces like screens or keyboards.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is significant for OpenHome as it can enhance user accessibility and engagement, allowing users to interact with their environments hands-free and in a more natural and intuitive manner. This model aligns with modern trends towards smart homes and integrated voice assistants.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, the voice-first concept manifests through various interfaces and interactions that allow users to control devices and access information using voice commands. This includes features like voice-activated home automation systems, conversational agents for customer support, and voice recognition capabilities that streamline user experiences.
