### definition
Voice-first refers to an approach in technology that prioritizes voice interactions as the primary means of communication with devices. In the context of OpenHome, it implies designing applications and interactions that are initiated and conducted primarily through voice commands.

### why-it-matters
Voice-first is crucial to OpenHome because it aligns with the platform's mission to create immersive and intuitive interactions between users and their devices. By leveraging voice as the primary interface, OpenHome enables a more natural, user-friendly experience, allowing users to engage with technology in a conversational manner.

### how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, voice-first principles manifest through the development of Personalities, which are AI agents equipped to understand and respond to voice commands. Users can create and modify these Personalities to include various Abilities—modular plugins that enable additional functionalities. The system responds to voice triggers, facilitating real-time dialogue and interaction without the need for traditional input methods.
