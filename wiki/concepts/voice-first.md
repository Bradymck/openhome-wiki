## definition
Voice-first technology refers to the primary use of voice as the main interface for interaction with devices and applications, emphasizing natural language processing and speech recognition.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, voice-first technology plays a critical role in creating more intuitive, hands-free user experiences. It allows users to interact with their environment and access information seamlessly, which is vital for the modern demands of connectivity and automation.

## how-it-manifests
Voice-first capabilities can be seen in various aspects of the OpenHome platform, including voice-activated commands for controlling smart home devices, accessing services, and retrieving information. This technology enhances user engagement by allowing for natural language queries and commands, significantly improving user accessibility and comfort.
