## definition
Voice-first refers to a design and interaction paradigm that prioritizes voice as the primary medium for user input and system output. This approach emphasizes using natural language processing to enable users to interact with technology through spoken commands, often fostering a more intuitive and seamless experience.

## why-it-matters
The voice-first approach is vital to OpenHome as it aligns with the growing trend towards more accessible and user-friendly technology. By leveraging voice as a primary interface, OpenHome aims to enhance user engagement, inclusivity, and convenience, making technology more approachable for diverse demographics, including those with accessibility needs.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first capabilities are integrated into various features such as voice-controlled home automation, virtual assistants, and interactive applications. Users can execute commands, access information, and manage devices through voice interactions, simplifying their experience and streamlining tasks in their daily lives.
