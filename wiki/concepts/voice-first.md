### definition
Voice-first refers to a design philosophy that prioritizes voice interaction as the primary means of communication with technology, enabling users to engage with applications and devices using spoken language.

### why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is relevant to OpenHome because it enhances user accessibility and engagement, allowing for hands-free operation and a more natural interaction with digital environments. This approach aligns with the modern trend of increasing reliance on voice assistants and smart devices in everyday life.

### how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first concepts can manifest through various features such as voice recognition capabilities, voice-activated commands for controlling home environments, and integration with voice assistants that facilitate smooth interactions between users and applications.
