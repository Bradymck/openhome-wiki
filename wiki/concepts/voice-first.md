## definition
Voice-first refers to an interaction model where users engage with technology primarily through voice commands rather than traditional interfaces like keyboard and screen. This approach emphasizes natural language processing and speech recognition to provide seamless, hands-free interactions.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, adopting a voice-first approach allows for more intuitive user experiences. As smart home technology evolves, integrating voice interaction enhances accessibility and convenience, making it easier for users to manage their environments and access services hands-free.

## how-it-manifests
The voice-first concept appears in OpenHome through voice-enabled features that allow users to control smart devices, access information, and perform tasks using spoken commands. This includes functionalities like setting alarms, adjusting settings, and retrieving information without needing to interact with a physical interface.
