### definition
Voice-first refers to a design philosophy and technological approach that prioritizes voice interaction as the primary means of user engagement and interface. This concept emphasizes the use of voice commands to interact with devices, applications, and services, allowing for hands-free and more intuitive user experiences.

### why-it-matters
The voice-first approach is relevant to OpenHome as it enhances accessibility and user interaction, making it easier for individuals to engage with technology in their daily lives. By leveraging voice commands, OpenHome provides users with a seamless and efficient way to control their environment, access information, and perform tasks without needing to rely on traditional input methods like keyboards or touchscreens.

### how-it-manifests
Within the OpenHome platform, voice-first capabilities manifest through voice-activated features and integrations that allow users to interact with various applications and services. This could include smart home controls, information retrieval, and task management, all executed through natural language processing technology that understands user commands.
