## definition
Voice-first refers to a technological approach where voice serves as the primary interface for interacting with applications and devices. In a voice-first environment, users communicate through spoken commands, enhancing the way they engage with technology.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first interaction is crucial for OpenHome because it aligns with the mission of making technology accessible and intuitive for users. By prioritizing voice as the main mode of communication, OpenHome seeks to create a seamless and immersive user experience that allows for natural and engaging interactions with voice AI agents.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first functionality appears through the development of conversational AI agents known as Personalities. These agents utilize voice interactions to execute tasks, respond to queries, and engage in dialogue, making the experience user-friendly and efficient. Abilities, which are plugins that enhance agent skills, are activated through voice commands, demonstrating the platform’s commitment to voice-first technology.
