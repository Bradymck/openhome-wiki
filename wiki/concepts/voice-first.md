## definition
Voice-first refers to a design philosophy where voice interaction is prioritized as the primary means of communication between users and technology. In this approach, applications are optimized for spoken language, allowing users to engage with devices through natural voice commands rather than traditional graphical interfaces.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first interactions are significant for OpenHome because they enhance user accessibility and engagement. By making AI-powered applications primarily voice-operated, OpenHome aims to provide intuitive, human-like interactions that simplify technology use and foster deeper connections between users and their devices.

## how-it-manifests
Within the OpenHome platform, the voice-first concept is manifested through the creation of conversational AI agents known as Personalities. These Personalities utilize voice interactions to communicate with users. Additionally, modular voice plugins (Abilities) enable these Personalities to perform various tasks, responding to voice commands while offering a seamless user experience.
