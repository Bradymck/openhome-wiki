## definition
Voice-first refers to an approach in which voice interaction is the primary method of engagement between users and technology. In this context, applications and devices are designed to prioritize spoken commands and responses, allowing for more natural and intuitive user experiences.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is central to OpenHome's mission of revolutionizing human-technology interaction. By focusing on voice, OpenHome aims to create immersive, intuitive, and engaging experiences that enhance everyday life and enable users to connect with their devices in meaningful ways.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, the voice-first approach is implemented through the development of conversational AI agents known as Personalities. These agents leverage the capabilities of Abilities—modular voice plugins that expand their functions—allowing for various interactions such as playing music, controlling smart home devices, and engaging in conversations. Users can trigger these Abilities using hotwords during voice interactions, showcasing the seamless integration of voice as the primary interface.
