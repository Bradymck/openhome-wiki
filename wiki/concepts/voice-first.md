### definition
Voice-first refers to a design and interaction paradigm where voice commands are the primary mode of engagement between users and technology. This approach prioritizes natural language processing and speech recognition, enabling users to perform tasks through verbal communication rather than traditional interfaces like touch or click.

### why-it-matters
Voice-first technologies are crucial for OpenHome as they provide a more intuitive and accessible user experience. This paradigm allows for hands-free interaction, making technology usable for individuals with varying physical abilities and enhancing usability in contexts where visual attention is limited.

### how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first functionality is integrated across various components, enabling users to navigate the system, control devices, and access information through simple voice commands. The platform may utilize virtual assistants, smart speakers, or voice-enabled applications, enhancing overall user engagement and satisfaction.
