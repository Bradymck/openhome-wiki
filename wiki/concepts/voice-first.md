## definition
Voice-first refers to an approach and design principle in technology that prioritizes voice as the primary interaction method between users and devices. In voice-first applications, users communicate through spoken commands and responses, enabling a more natural and intuitive user experience.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is significant to OpenHome because it aligns with the mission to revolutionize human interaction with technology. By emphasizing voice interactions, OpenHome aims to create an immersive and accessible AI-powered experience that integrates seamlessly into users' daily lives.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first interactions are realized through the development of Personalities, which are AI agents that respond to user inputs. These agents utilize Abilities—modular voice plugins—that enhance their functionality. Users can trigger these Abilities through natural conversation and hotwords, making the interaction feel fluid and organic.
