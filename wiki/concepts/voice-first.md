## definition
Voice-first refers to an interface design philosophy that prioritizes voice interactions as the primary means of communication with technology. This approach allows users to engage with applications and devices primarily through spoken commands rather than traditional input methods like keyboards or touchscreens.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology is significant for OpenHome as it enhances accessibility and convenience for users. By enabling hands-free interaction, the platform can cater to a broader audience, including those with disabilities and those who prefer multitasking. This aligns with OpenHome's vision of creating user-friendly, innovative experiences.

## how-it-manifests
Voice-first technology appears within the OpenHome platform through its integration of voice recognition systems and natural language processing (NLP). Users can issue commands, ask questions, and retrieve information using their voice, streamlining their interactions with the platform. This functionality can be found in various features such as smart home management, information retrieval, and personalized assistance.
