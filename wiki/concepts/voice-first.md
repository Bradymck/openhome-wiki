## definition
Voice-first refers to the design philosophy that prioritizes voice interactions as the primary means of engaging with technology, allowing users to control devices and access information through spoken commands. This concept is foundational in creating more natural and intuitive human-computer interactions.

## why-it-matters
Voice-first technology enhances user experience by providing an alternative to traditional input methods, such as touch and typing. In the context of OpenHome, it democratizes access to AI-powered applications, making them more accessible to users who may not be comfortable with conventional interfaces. This aligns with OpenHome's mission to revolutionize technology interactions.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first capabilities manifest through the development of Personalities, which are AI agents that utilize voice to communicate with users. These agents can be equipped with Abilities—modular plugins that enable them to perform specific tasks in response to voice queries. Users trigger these Abilities through voice commands, facilitating a seamless interaction with their smart devices.
