## definition
Voice-first refers to a design and interaction paradigm that prioritizes voice as the primary method for user engagement with technology. In a voice-first approach, applications are developed primarily for voice interactions, allowing users to communicate and interact with devices using natural language.

## why-it-matters
The voice-first concept is crucial for OpenHome as it aligns with the platform's mission to create intuitive, human-like interactions with AI. By making voice the central mode of communication, OpenHome can enhance the user experience, ensuring that engagements with technology are seamless and natural. This approach empowers users to connect with their devices in meaningful ways, simplifying complex tasks through conversational interfaces.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, voice-first interactions are embodied through Personalities, which are customizable AI agents designed to engage with users through conversation. These Personalities can respond to user input, manage smart home devices, play music, and much more, all triggered by voice commands. The integration of voice recognition technology allows for real-time processing of spoken queries, making it easier for users to access functionality without needing traditional input methods.
