### definition
Web3 Native refers to applications, platforms, and services that are built on decentralized networks and utilize blockchain technology. These entities prioritize user ownership, privacy, and the ability for individuals to interact and transact without intermediaries.

### why-it-matters
Web3 Native concepts are crucial for OpenHome as they encapsulate the ethos of decentralization and user empowerment that the platform aims to promote. By leveraging blockchain technology, OpenHome can ensure greater transparency, security, and user control over personal data.

### how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, the Web3 Native approach can be seen through various functionalities that allow users to manage their identities, control access to their data, and engage with the platform in a peer-to-peer manner. These features support the development of a community-driven ecosystem that aligns with the principles of Web3.
