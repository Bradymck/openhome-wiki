## definition
Web3-native refers to applications, platforms, and systems built using decentralized technologies such as blockchain, primarily designed to operate in a user-centric manner without reliance on traditional centralized authorities.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native allows for enhanced user control over data and privacy, encouraging a shift toward more autonomous digital interactions. This aligns with the goals of decentralization and user empowerment essential to OpenHome's vision.

## how-it-manifests
The web3-native concept is integrated within OpenHome through its decentralized architecture, which utilizes blockchain technology to provide transparent user interactions, enable secure transactions, and facilitate the creation and management of user-owned assets in various applications offered on the platform.
