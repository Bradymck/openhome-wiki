## definition
Web3-native refers to applications, services, and platforms that are built on decentralized technologies like blockchain. This approach prioritizes user ownership, privacy, and interoperability, fundamentally shifting the way digital interactions are conducted compared to traditional web technologies.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native is crucial as it aligns with the principles of decentralization and user empowerment. It enables users to have control over their data and interactions, fostering a more secure and transparent environment for community engagement. This approach can enhance user trust and participation within the platform.

## how-it-manifests
OpenHome manifests the web3-native concept through features that utilize decentralized identity systems, smart contracts for transactions, and peer-to-peer interactions. Users can engage with the platform in a way that emphasizes control over their own digital assets and identities, and transactions are transparent and immutable due to the underlying blockchain technology.
