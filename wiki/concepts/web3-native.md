## definition
Web3-native refers to applications, platforms, and systems that are built on decentralized technologies, particularly those that leverage blockchain, peer-to-peer networking, and community-driven governance. These entities prioritize user control, privacy, and direct ownership of digital assets.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native is significant because it empowers users with greater control over their data, enhances privacy, and encourages community collaboration. This aligns with OpenHome’s goal of creating immersive and user-centric AI interactions, where users can engage with technology in a manner that respects their autonomy.

## how-it-manifests
Web3-native concepts appear within the OpenHome platform through functionalities that enable users to create, share, and monetize Abilities and Personalities in a decentralized manner. Users can directly contribute to the ecosystem without intermediaries, fostering a community-centric approach to development.
