## definition
Web3-native refers to applications, services, or platforms that are built using decentralized technologies such as blockchain and that prioritize user ownership, privacy, and autonomy. These platforms leverage smart contracts and decentralized protocols to create a more open and user-centric internet experience.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, web3-native concepts are crucial as they empower users with greater control over their data, enabling them to interact with the platform without intermediary entities. This aligns with OpenHome's goals of fostering user autonomy and trust.

## how-it-manifests
Web3-native principles manifest on the OpenHome platform through features like decentralized identity verification, smart contracts for transaction management, and protocols that ensure data ownership remains with the user. This establishes a transparent and fair ecosystem for all participants in the OpenHome environment.
