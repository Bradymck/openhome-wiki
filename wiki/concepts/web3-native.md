### definition
Web3-native refers to applications and services that are built and operate within the decentralized framework of the web3 ecosystem, leveraging blockchain technology and decentralized protocols for enhanced user control, ownership, and transparency.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native allows for a more decentralized and user-centric approach to the development of AI-powered voice applications. This encourages innovations that align with the values of openness, user empowerment, and transparency in interactions.

### how-it-manifests
Web3-native concepts manifest within the OpenHome platform as users create and share decentralized applications for voice AI. These applications can utilize blockchain-based functionalities such as secure data sharing, user ownership of data, and governance mechanisms through decentralized protocols, enhancing user trust and engagement.
