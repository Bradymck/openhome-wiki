## definition
Web3 native refers to technologies and applications that are specifically built on decentralized networks, including blockchain and peer-to-peer systems. These applications leverage the unique capabilities of Web3 to promote user ownership, data privacy, and decentralized governance.

## why-it-matters
Web3 native concepts align with OpenHome's commitment to providing a user-centric platform where individuals have control over their data and can participate in decentralized governance. This model enhances trust and security, which are paramount for the community-driven aspects of OpenHome.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, Web3 native elements may appear in features such as decentralized identity management, where users manage their profiles without a central authority, or in transaction systems that use blockchain for secure property ownership transfers. Furthermore, governance models might incorporate decentralized decision-making processes where users can vote on important platform changes.
