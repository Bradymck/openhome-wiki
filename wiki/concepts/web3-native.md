## definition
Web3-native refers to applications and platforms that are built using decentralized protocols and technologies, prioritizing user ownership, privacy, and data sovereignty. These technologies leverage blockchain and distributed networks to create a more open and equitable digital ecosystem.

## why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native means that the platform is designed to integrate with decentralized systems, allowing users to have greater control over their data and interactions with AI. This approach aligns with the broader movement towards decentralization in technology and ensures that users are empowered in their interactions.

## how-it-manifests
OpenHome incorporates web3-native principles by enabling the creation of AI agents and abilities that utilize decentralized protocols for data management and interaction. This may involve features that allow users to manage their personal data independently and interact with smart home devices in a secure, private manner.
