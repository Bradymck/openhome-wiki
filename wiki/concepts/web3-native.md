## definition
Web3-native refers to applications, services, and platforms that are built on decentralized technologies like blockchain, enabling users to have greater control over their digital identities, data, and assets. These systems leverage smart contracts, decentralized finance (DeFi), and tokens to create a more user-centric online experience.

## why-it-matters
Web3-native solutions are relevant to OpenHome because they promote a more equitable internet, allowing users to own and manage their own data and interactions, thus aligning with the core philosophy of decentralization and user empowerment that OpenHome aims to achieve.

## how-it-manifests
In the OpenHome platform, web3-native concepts can manifest through features such as decentralized identity verification, user-controlled data storage, and smart contract-driven interactions, all of which empower users to engage with digital content in a secure and transparent manner.
