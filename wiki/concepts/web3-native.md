### definition
Web3-native refers to applications, platforms, and technologies that are built fundamentally on decentralized web protocols, utilizing blockchain technology and enabling peer-to-peer interactions without central authorities.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3-native enables the platform to empower users with greater control over their data, privacy, and interactions. This fosters a more democratic technology ecosystem, reducing reliance on traditional intermediaries while enhancing trust and security.

### how-it-manifests
The web3-native aspect of OpenHome is evident in its architecture that may leverage decentralized networks or protocols to facilitate communication and transactions among voice AI agents. This enables users to own their data and interact with services directly, promoting collaboration and innovation in the development of AI abilities.
