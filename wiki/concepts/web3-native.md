## definition
Web3 native refers to applications and services that are built using decentralized protocols and technologies, particularly those that leverage blockchain and smart contracts. These applications prioritize user ownership, interoperability, and transparency, fostering a more user-centric internet experience.

## why-it-matters
The concept of Web3 native is highly relevant to OpenHome as it aligns with the platform's mission to be an open-source, decentralized environment for voice AI applications. By adopting Web3 principles, OpenHome can enable developers to create voice applications that not only enhance functionality but also empower users with control over their data and interactions.

## how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, the Web3 native approach may manifest through integrated smart contracts for transactions, decentralized identity management for users, and tokenized systems that incentivize contributions from developers. By incorporating Web3 native features, OpenHome can enhance security, user trust, and overall engagement on the platform.
