### definition
Web3-native refers to applications and platforms that are built using decentralized technologies and principles that define the Web3 movement. This includes the use of blockchain, smart contracts, and decentralized protocols that empower users to interact with technology in a peer-to-peer manner without intermediaries.

### why-it-matters
The concept of being web3-native is significant to OpenHome as it aligns with the platform's mission to promote decentralization and user empowerment. By integrating web3 principles, OpenHome can facilitate a more secure, transparent, and user-controlled environment for its AI-powered applications.

### how-it-manifests
In OpenHome, the web3-native concept appears in the way participants can build and deploy decentralized applications (dApps) or connect their AI agents to decentralized networks. This may also include using blockchain for transaction logging, identity verification, and creating more robust smart home integrations that emphasize user data privacy and ownership.
