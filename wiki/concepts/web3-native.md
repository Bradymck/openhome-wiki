### definition
Web3 native refers to applications and platforms that are built specifically to leverage decentralized technologies, primarily the blockchain. These applications prioritize user autonomy, data ownership, and create incentives for community-oriented governance and collaboration.

### why-it-matters
In the context of OpenHome, being web3 native matters because it aligns with the mission to provide users with control over their data and interactions. It opens up opportunities for innovative integrations, enabling users to engage with smart home technologies in decentralized ways.

### how-it-manifests
The concept of being web3 native may manifest in OpenHome through features that allow for decentralized data storage, user ownership of digital identities, and the integration of cryptocurrency or tokens for transactions and rewards within the platform.
